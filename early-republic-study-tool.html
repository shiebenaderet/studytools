Science-Backed Study Tips

✓ Spaced Repetition

Review material at increasing intervals: after 1 day, 3 days, 1 week, and 2 weeks. This tool tracks your vocabulary mastery—use it to review harder terms more frequently.

✓ Active Recall

Test yourself instead of just re-reading. Use the flashcards and practice tests to force your brain to retrieve information. This is more effective than passive review.

✓ Focused Study Sessions

Use the timer for 25-50 minute focused sessions with 5-10 minute breaks. This is the Pomodoro Technique, proven to improve concentration and learning.

✓ Mix It Up (Interleaving)

Don't study one topic for hours. Instead, alternate between vocabulary, timeline, and practice questions. Your brain learns better when challenged to switch between concepts.

✓ Elaborate & Connect

When learning something new, ask "Why?" and "How does this connect to what I already know?" Use the connections question to see how concepts relate to each other.

✓ Teach Someone Else

The best way to master material is to explain it to someone else. Study with a partner and quiz each other. Your short answer responses are great practice for this.

✓ Get Enough Sleep

Your brain consolidates memories during sleep. Don't cram the night before the test—spread studying over several days and get good sleep before the big day.
