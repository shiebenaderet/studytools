# Source materials — what's where

The `_source-materials/` folder has TWO types of PDFs for each chapter:

| Workbook (activities) | Textbook (prose) |
|---|---|
| `19 Worlds of North and South.pdf` | `CH_19_The Worlds of North and South.pdf` |
| `20 African Americans.pdf` | `CH_20_African Americans in the Mid-1800s.pdf` |
| `21 Dividing Nation.pdf` | `CH_21_A Dividing Nation.pdf` |
| (no separate workbook found) | `CH_22_The Civil War.pdf` |

The **textbook** PDFs are the ones we want for Phase 3 — they contain the *History Alive! The United States Through Industrialism* lesson prose at 8th-grade reading level. The workbook PDFs are the activity scaffolds (Preview/Reading Notes/Processing) that students fill out in class — useful for understanding the chapter framing but not source material.

## Highlighted SS Vocabulary in each textbook chapter

Each textbook lesson opens with a "Social Studies Vocabulary" sidebar listing the terms the chapter teaches explicitly. These don't always match the teaching outline word-for-word; the outline often picks teacher-emphasized terms that go beyond the textbook's highlighted set.

### Ch 20 — African Americans in the Mid-1800s
Textbook highlights: discrimination, Nat Turner's Rebellion, oppression, racism, segregation, Underground Railroad

Outline calls out: Slave codes, Overseer, Spirituals, "Invisible institution", Underground Railroad, Abolitionist, Resistance (overt vs. everyday), Field hand / House servant, Slave family, African retentions

→ Major term-set divergence. Plan should reconcile these. Discrimination/oppression/racism/segregation are NOT in the outline but ARE in the textbook. We need to decide whether to add them as must-know terms (textbook says they're key) or leave them as encounter-tier.

### Ch 21 — A Dividing Nation
Textbook highlights: Compromise of 1850, Dred Scott decision, fugitive, Kansas-Nebraska Act, Lincoln-Douglas debates, Missouri Compromise, Union, Wilmot Proviso

Outline calls out: Missouri Compromise, Wilmot Proviso, Compromise of 1850, Fugitive Slave Law, popular sovereignty, Uncle Tom's Cabin, Kansas-Nebraska Act/Bleeding Kansas, Dred Scott decision, Lincoln-Douglas debates/Harpers Ferry/1860 election, Secession

→ Two textbook-only terms not in outline: **fugitive** (textbook-defined as "a person who flees or tries to escape, e.g. from slavery") and **Union** (textbook-defined as "the United States as one nation united under a single government"). Both are encounter-tier candidates per user decision.

### Ch 22 — The Civil War
Textbook highlights: Appomattox Court House, civil war, Confederacy, Emancipation Proclamation, Gettysburg Address, habeas corpus

Outline calls out: Union/Confederacy, Fort Sumter, Bull Run, Antietam, Emancipation Proclamation, Gettysburg, Vicksburg, 54th Massachusetts/Fort Wagner, Total war/Sherman's March, Appomattox

→ Habeas corpus is textbook-only and worth adding (encounter). "Civil war" itself is highlighted but already implied by the unit title; skip.

## Bull Run question resolved
Ch 22 textbook devotes its whole section 2 to "Bull Run: A Great Awakening" — first major battle, Stonewall Jackson's name origin, "On to Richmond," Rose Greenhow's spying. **Bull Run becomes a must-know term.**

## Note for Phase 2 vocab authoring

When authoring new term definitions, the textbook prose is now the source. Scan it for the natural definition the textbook uses, paraphrase to ~15-20 words, attach the textbook's example/context as the `example` field.
